Nikola Jokic Carving a Path to Back-to-Back MVP Awards

Monday April 4th, 2022.

Nikola Jokic holds the lead for this season’s MVP race. The “joker” has the highest player efficiency rating (PER) of all the candidates for the award and he is playing a better season than his MVP season. Jokic’s PER is not only the highest of all the candidates, but it is also the highest PER ever recorded. Despite playing the season with Jamal Murray and Michael Porter Jr. sidelined, Nikola Jokic has managed to lead the Denver Nuggets to the fifth seed in the western conference.

Over the past two seasons, Jokic has proven to be a consistent player, but that is not enough for many fans. The MVP award is usually presented to someone on championship acquiring teams, which is not the Denver Nuggets. Despite Jokic’s insane numbers, which include double-double averages, analysts degrade these numbers to inflation from the lack of scoring competition with Murray and Porter Jr. being on the bench. These challenges along with lack of media coverage for Jokic are what place him behind Joel Embiid and Giannis Antetokounmpo in the MVP race. However, according to Coach Michael Malone, “if Nikola Jokic isn’t the MVP, who is?”

Miriam Akhnoukh, an avid sports fan, and a sophomore at the University of Illinois, finds this topic to be of little interest for one sole reason, “it’s all about publicity.” Akhnoukh continued, “Nikola Jokic is the clear candidate for MVP, but Joel Embiid and Giannis Antetokounmpo are the players who bring in views and views equate to money. The Nuggets do not have as big of an audience as the Milwaukee Bucks and the Philadelphia 76ers. In fact, that is the reason Nikola Jokic was an MVP without a Christmas day game.” Akhnoukh’s statements, however, are not far-fetched. In many casual conversations I have had about the Denver Nuggets, I have heard statements like, “they just are not fun to watch,” and “Jokic is a good player, but the team is not fun to watch.” Alexandra Hosny, a sophomore at the University of Illinois, agreed with both these statements and Akhnoukh’s statements.

Although analysts and viewers degrade Jokic’s stats to inflation, Stat Muse- an artificial intelligence company leading in conversational search for sports stats and information—notes these impressive numbers. According to State Muse, “Nikola Jokic is having a season we’ve never seen before in NBA history. He is averaging 26.0 PPG, 13.8 RPG, 7.9 APG. No other player has even had a 25/12/6 season before.” These stats were back from February 17th, currently Jokic is averaging 26.8 PPG, 13.7 RPG, and 8 APG, which has never been seen before.

Since the start of his career, Nikola Jokic has been underappreciated because he was never viewed as the most athletic player. Jokic currently stands at 7 feet tall and 284 pounds. Despite his physique, Jokic is not able to dunk which is considered a standard skill for someone at his height. Small disadvantages like these are what set him apart from the star players of the NBA. Coach Michael Malone, the Nuggets head coach, has a lot to say about this topic. Malone says, “He played in all 72 games last year, played at an MVP level. For him to do what he’s doing this year, once again, and he won it last year, and all the numbers and stats and metrics you come up with, he’s having a better season than he had last year when he won it. Our team, with a lot of guys out, is playing at one of the highest levels in the NBA. Come on, man. What are we talking about?” Coach Malone believes that Jokic’s achievements do not get talked about enough because he is not, “prolific athlete that a lot of these guys are.”

When posed with the thoughts of Coach Malone, Hosny said, “he is not wrong.” She continued to say, “it’s hard to appreciate Jokic’s talent and level of athleticism when the idea of an athlete is someone like Lebron James who spends millions of dollars a year maintaining his physique.”

Regardless of all this controversy surrounding Nikola Jokic and the MVP award, when the MVP candidate is asked about what back-to-back MVP awards would mean to him, he says, “I don’t know.” Jokic states, “it doesn’t mean anything special. I am just trying to compete to win games.”
